This Week's Article · Miami vs. New York

The Luxury Capital Moved South: Miami Overtakes New York in Million-Dollar Listings

For the first time in nearly a decade, New York is no longer America's capital of million-dollar listings — Miami is. And the metro that took the crown runs straight through Boca Raton and Delray Beach.

Realtor.com's 2026 luxury outlook puts the Miami–Fort Lauderdale–West Palm Beach metro at 10,513 active million-dollar listings at year-end 2025 — decisively ahead of New York's 9,216. Behind the crossover: 26.3% of Miami's luxury demand comes from the New York metro (more than the next eight feeder markets combined), and Florida captured 21% of all foreign U.S. homebuyers — the highest share of any state.

What it means inside the gates: more seven-figure competition rewards pricing discipline for sellers — and the migration is already on the closing sheets, with Boca West at 89 sales this year, Broken Sound at 64, and the Polo Club at 55 across the 21 communities we track.
